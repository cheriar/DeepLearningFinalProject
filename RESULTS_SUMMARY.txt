========================================================================
PROPOSED MODIFICATION: Adaptive Frequency Cutoff
========================================================================

Baseline Approach (Paper):
- Fixed σ = 0.5 for all images (one-size-fits-all)
- Trade-off: higher σ → better robustness, lower σ → better clean accuracy

Our Adaptive Approach:
- Per-image σ ∈ [0.3, 0.7] based on spectral entropy
- Simple images (low frequency complexity) → σ = 0.6-0.7 (more augmentation)
- Complex images (high frequency detail) → σ = 0.3-0.4 (preserve detail)

Implementation:
✓ Spectral entropy computation (utils/adaptive_cutoff.py)
✓ Adaptive sigma integration (datasets/APR.py)
✓ Maintains generalization (content-based, not identity-based)

Expected Improvements:
- Clean accuracy: +0.5-1.5% (better detail preservation for complex images)
- mCE: -0.5 to -1.5 points (stronger augmentation for simple images)
- Better semantic preservation across image complexity spectrum

Scientific Justification:
- Paper showed σ trade-off exists but used fixed compromise value
- We extend by making trade-off image-adaptive rather than global
- Analogous to adaptive learning rates vs fixed learning rate
